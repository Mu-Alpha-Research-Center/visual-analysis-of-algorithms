Introduction

  • Interviewing is a skill and anyone can learn it.

Tip #1: Use recruiters to your advantage

  • The recruiter wants to work with you. They know that you probably have poor interview prep skill, and are happy to help fix that flaw. Why discard strong candidates who can't interview well? It's not in their best interest. It's better for them to help candidates show their best selves when interviewing.
  • Ask them questions like:
    • What should I do to prepare for the interview?
    • What are the company values that would be good to highlight during the interview?

Tip #2: Go to real interviews for practice

Learn to deal with pressure

  • You need to build up your interviewing skills. LeetCode is great, but it doesn't come close to the real thing.
  • Real interviews teach you how to deal with the pressure. The first interview will kick your ass. So will the second. But once you get a few under your belt you'll get used to the adrenaline rush.
  • I've found that I perform the best in the interviews where I don't care about the outcome. I'm much more relaxed and I can think faster. Now, even in important interviews I try to convince myself that I don't really care.

Learn to answer the more ambiguous questions

  • Write down all the questions you were asked, and consider how you could have responded better. Focus on the behavioral and design ones which ahve no clear right answer. Are there stories from your life you could have referenced? Wait a few days and look at them again. You'll find better answers.

Stay open to serendipity

  • As you do these interviews, you may discover that the "practice" company is actually interesting after all. Recruiters count on that.

Tip #3: Be open to learning during the interview

  • Since then I've stopped hesitating before asking any question in an interview. Let your curiosity run free! Don't cage it! And as an interviewer, I can attest: sincere interest is always a good sign.

Tip #4: Keep your skills sharp even when you’re not job hunting

  • James Whittaker recommends trying to get a job offer every year, just to make sure you can. It's an extremely liberating feeling knowing that even if you lose your job you'll be able to find another one quickly.

Conclusion

  • What do all these tactics have in common? They eliminate your fear. The fear that holds you back from letting your best self out. Do them often enough and interview rooms will transform from a threatening jungle into Tarzan's playground.
